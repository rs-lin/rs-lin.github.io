I am a second year statistics PhD student at George Mason University (GMU). I work with Dr. Jonathan Auerbach and Dr. David Kepplinger . The goal of my research is to develop data analytical tools to handle outliers and quantify uncertainty for citizen science data. My research interests focus on robust and non-parametric statistical methodologies and applications.
Before coming to Mason, I obtained a Bachelor's in mathematics and a minor in German from New York University (NYU). In my junior year, I lived in Germany for one semester as an exchange student. After graduating from NYU, I worked as a machine learning engineer for a FinTech startup, where I became familiar with the financial market and chatbot development.
